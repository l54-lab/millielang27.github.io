Gulf Coast Gauntlet
Quick Summary
The Situation

You're flying a CubCrafters NX Cub from 2J9 to KPGD, following the Gulf Coast. After nearly two hours of dodging showers, you reach the Tampa area with deteriorating visibility ahead. Tampa's Class B airspace sits between you and home. The only airport behind you is IFR in showers. Fuel: 4+ hours remaining.

Your Options
1Fly under the Class B shelf and press on toward KPGD
2Call Tampa Approach, request Class B clearance, and proceed
✓Land at the nearest airport and wait for conditions to improve
4Circle in the current area and wait for weather to pass
What the Experts Said

The panel split between requesting Class B clearance and landing — both were considered acceptable. The consensus: with fuel available and an instrument rating in your pocket, there is no reason to duck under the shelf in deteriorating conditions. Options 2 and 3 both keep you safe. Option 1 does not.

Key Takeaways
  • Your instrument rating is a tool — use it. Requesting Class B clearance gives you radar separation in conditions where you need it.
  • Fuel is time. With 4+ hours remaining, there is no pressure to press on. Land and wait.
  • The ceiling and visibility at decision time were marginal VFR at best. Descending further to stay clear of clouds compounds the risk.
